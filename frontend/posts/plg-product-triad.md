9: 
10: In a high performance organization that is following the Product Led Growth (PLG) model, the product
11: is at the very center of everything, with the user being the center of the product. Lean management
12: fits extremely well with the PLG model as it looks to maximize value delivery to the user while
13: minimizing waste in the delivery process. Every department, team, and individual shares the same
14: goal of improving the product and each one brings a unique and important perspective in this
15: alignment; playing a specific role in the makeup of the organization.
16: 
17: 
18: ## The Core Product Groups
19: ### Product
20: 
21: Product is responsible for the value that is delivered to the end user through the product. They are
22: the collection point for all end user feedback. Their primary responsibility is to collect all of
23: this feedback, and synthesize it to a problem definition. They also lead the planning on what
24: metrics need to be collected to verify the problem and calculate the value of solving the problem
25: for the end user. Product then uses the value calculation to and rank problem definitions against
26: one another. All types of reasons of why something should be done in the product should be captured
27: in metrics used to calculate the value. No product decision, whether it leads to financial growth or
28: not, is made without the data in support.
29: 
30: 
31: ### Desgin
32: 
33: Design owns and is responsible for designing the solution to a defined problem that Product deems
34: the highest value to end users. Solutions and alternatives are developed and user feedback is
35: gathered to validate the solution. The size and scope of these problems could vary greatly; from a
36: small change in the corner of a settings page to a complete user experience overhaul. If the
37: proposed solution is large functionality change that requires significant investment, user feedback
38: via user research should be done previous to implementation. However, if the scope of the solution
39: is not large, user feedback comes post implementation and be naturally added to the Product user feedback collection strategy.
40: If A/B testing is applicable, it would be used here to enable
41: in-product user feedback. 
42: 
43: Secondarily, Design uses their user experience expertise to help support Product with user data by
44: providing user research to help measure and analyze product in support of defining user problems and
45: prioritizing the defined problems. 
46: 
47: 
48: ### Engineering
49: 
50: The primary responsibility of Engineering Development is to implement the solution that Design has
51: developed. Their secondary responsibility is to use their technical expertise to support Design with
52: review of solutions and alternatives to Product's defined problems. Design leans heavily on
53: Engineering for the feasibility and complexity of a solution and Engineering is responsible for
54: providing feedback to assist with minimizing technical complexity. Minimizing complexity is
55: important to contribute to the overall Lean goals of maximizing value delivery while minimizing
56: waste. Technical complexity is slow to develop and test which makes it the enemy of Lean since it
57: causes friction in the fast delivery of value. Complex solutions are also prone to rework which
58: increases waste. 
59: 
60: 
61: ## Culture of Experimentation
62: 
63: If the organization's engineering practices support a culture of experimentation and iteration, one of the important
64: user data collection streams comes from in-product. Increasing the velocity of delivering a minimum valuable product
65: into the hands of the users to gain feedback enables higher velocity of value learning. The primary business goal of a
66: product is to delight and deliver value to the users. Feedback on already-delivered product capabilities leads to faster
67: refinement of what is valuable to the end user: what is worth investing in and what is not. This is the secret to
68: leading the marketplace. Enabling the culture of experimentation, iteration, and high velocity are the outcomes of
69: practicing DevOps.
70: 
71: The organization structure should be such that it promotes PLG and Lean management. The Lean
72: management principles should be applied to the communication channel creation as well: maximize the
73: delivery of value in communication while minimizing waste of unneeded communication. Organization
74: communication is the hardest problem to solve. [Conway's
75: Law](https://www.melconway.com/Home/Committees_Paper.html), the [Inverse Conway Maneuver
76: reference](https://yoan-thirion.gitbook.io/knowledge-base/xtrem-reading/resources/book-notes/team-topologies#conways-law)
77: to the findings in [Accelerate](https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/accelerate/9781457191435/) in
78: relation to the Inverse Conway Maneuver and the [implications of executing such a maneuver
79: incorrectly](https://medium.com/@Cyrdup/icm-1-say-no-to-the-inverse-conway-maneuver-6672ba2373cb).
80: Another article will be written around Conway's Law and the Inverse Conway Maneuver.

85: In PLG organizations, the Core product groups each have complimentary roles and responsibilities to play in order to
86: be the most effective. Each business function relies heavily on each other to make up the whole of the core product 
87: groups to optimize the Lean management goals: maximizing value delivery to the end user and minimizing waste in the
88: delivery.
